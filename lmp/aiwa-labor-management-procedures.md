# AI West Africa (AIWA) Labor Management Procedures (LMP)

## 1. Introduction

### Purpose
This Labor Management Procedures (LMP) document establishes AIWA's framework for:
- employment standards;
- worker protections;
- labor procedures;
- workplace conduct expectations;
- occupational health and safety measures; and
- worker grievance procedures.

### Scope
This LMP applies to workers engaged by AIWA, including:
- employees;
- consultants;
- contracted workers;
- interns;
- temporary personnel; and
- volunteers (where treated as workers).

Programmatic safeguarding, school engagement, child protection, media ethics, and community engagement requirements are governed by separate institutional policies.

## 2. Organizational Overview

AIWA is a media and publishing organization that also supports educational and cultural programming, including national language and heritage initiatives and copyright/publishing representation.

## 3. Applicable Legal Framework

This LMP is guided by applicable labor requirements in The Gambia, including:
- Labour Act 2007;
- occupational health and safety obligations;
- anti-discrimination protections;
- employment contract requirements; and
- workers' compensation obligations.

Where activities are donor-funded, relevant labor requirements under the World Bank Environmental and Social Standard 2 (ESS2) may also apply.

## 4. Categories of Workers

### Direct Workers
Employees directly hired by AIWA.

### Contracted Workers
Consultants, facilitators, editors, trainers, media contractors, and other externally contracted personnel.

### Interns and Volunteers
Interns and volunteers are engaged under defined roles, supervision, and applicable code of conduct requirements.

### International Consultants
International consultants, where engaged, are subject to contract terms and this LMP's applicable provisions.

## 5. Key Labor Risks

### A. Workplace Harassment and Misconduct
- sexual harassment;
- bullying and intimidation;
- abuse of authority; and
- discrimination.

### B. Occupational Health and Safety
- office safety;
- travel and event safety;
- ergonomic risks;
- stress and burnout; and
- electrical/fire risks.

### C. Reputational and Ethical Conduct Risks
- misuse of confidential information;
- inappropriate public conduct;
- unauthorized communications; and
- conflicts of interest.

### D. Data and Information Security Risks
- protection of unpublished materials;
- secure handling of worker and operational data; and
- confidentiality obligations.

### E. Labor Compliance Risks
- unfair recruitment;
- unpaid labor;
- lack of contracts;
- overtime disputes; and
- unclear consultant arrangements.

## 6. Human Resource Policies and Procedures

### Recruitment and Hiring
AIWA applies equal opportunity, merit-based selection, and transparent hiring practices.

### Employment Contracts
Written terms should define duties, compensation, duration, leave, confidentiality, and termination conditions.

### Working Hours and Leave
Working time, annual leave, sick leave, maternity/paternity leave, and public holidays are managed in line with applicable legal and organizational requirements.

### Disciplinary Procedures
AIWA applies fair and documented disciplinary procedures with notice of concerns, opportunity to respond, and proportionate action aligned with contract terms and applicable law.

### Internships and Volunteer Engagement
Intern and volunteer roles are defined with clear supervision, expected conduct, and role limitations.

### Remote and Field Work
Where applicable, procedures cover travel authorization, safety expectations, equipment use, and confidentiality in remote/field settings.

## 7. Code of Conduct

All workers must maintain:
- respectful workplace behavior;
- anti-harassment and anti-discrimination conduct;
- confidentiality;
- ethical communications;
- conflict of interest disclosure/management;
- proper use of organizational property; and
- prohibition of exploitation and abuse.

Workers are additionally required to comply with AIWA safeguarding, child protection, media ethics, and community engagement policies where applicable.

## 8. Occupational Health and Safety (OHS)

AIWA maintains practical OHS procedures covering:
- safe office environment;
- emergency and fire safety procedures;
- incident reporting;
- first-aid arrangements;
- travel safety; and
- psychosocial wellbeing.

## 9. Grievance Mechanism

AIWA provides an employee-focused grievance process with:
- confidential complaints channels;
- anti-retaliation protections;
- reporting channels for HR and ethics/misconduct issues;
- timelines for review and response (including acknowledgement within 5 business days where feasible);
- escalation pathways; and
- appeals procedures.

## 10. Contractor and Consultant Management

AIWA requires contractors and consultants to:
- operate under written contracts;
- comply with workplace conduct standards;
- adhere to relevant AIWA policies; and
- maintain confidentiality.

## 11. Training and Awareness

AIWA provides role-appropriate training on:
- workplace conduct;
- anti-harassment;
- OHS;
- confidentiality;
- ethics; and
- data protection.

## 12. Monitoring and Compliance

AIWA monitors implementation through periodic tracking of:
- grievances;
- incident records;
- training completion;
- disciplinary matters; and
- contractor compliance.
